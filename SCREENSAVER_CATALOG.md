## After Dark (Berkeley Systems, Mac 1989 / Windows 1991)

The legendary screensaver collection that started it all. After Dark was introduced by Berkeley Systems in 1989 for Mac and 1991 for Windows, spawning hundreds of modules and becoming a cultural phenomenon. The founders (Joan Blades and Wes Boyd) later went on to create MoveOn.org.

## Technical Notes for Implementation

### Architecture Considerations
- Each screensaver should be a standalone module in `/screensavers/`
- Follow existing pattern: `init()`, `destroy()`, `animate()`
- Support both fullscreen and preview mode
- Canvas-based for performance
- Consider WebGL for 3D screensavers

### Legal Considerations
- After Dark is abandonware but trademarked imagery (toasters) may need artistic interpretation
- XScreenSaver is open source (MIT/X11 license) - can study implementations
- Johnny Castaway has open source reimplementations to reference
- Windows screensavers are part of the OS, implementations are original

### Resources for Research
- Internet Archive: Various After Dark collections
- jwz.org/xscreensaver - Official XScreenSaver with screenshots
- GitHub: Many open source reimplementations exist
- YouTube: Video captures of original screensavers for reference
